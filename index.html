About the Initiative

World Physiotherapy Day 2025 focuses on "Healthy Ageing" with a specific emphasis on preventing frailty and falls in older adults. This global initiative highlights the crucial role of physiotherapy in promoting active, independent living throughout the ageing process.

Through evidence-based interventions, exercise programs, and fall prevention strategies, physiotherapists worldwide are working to enhance mobility, strength, and balance in older populations, reducing the risk of frailty and fall-related injuries.

Why It Matters

Frailty Prevention

Frailty affects millions of older adults worldwide, leading to loss of independence and increased healthcare needs. Targeted exercise programs can significantly reduce frailty progression.

Fall Reduction

Falls are a leading cause of injury among older adults. Balance training and strength exercises can reduce fall risk by up to 40%, preventing fractures and other serious injuries.

Prevention Strategies

Strength Training Exercises

Regular strength training helps maintain muscle mass, bone density, and functional ability. Focus on major muscle groups with exercises like squats, leg presses, and resistance band activities at least twice weekly.

Balance Training

Balance exercises such as single-leg stands, heel-to-toe walking, and Tai Chi can significantly improve stability and reduce fall risk. Practice these daily for best results.

Nutrition for Healthy Ageing

Adequate protein intake, vitamin D, calcium, and proper hydration are essential for maintaining muscle mass, bone health, and overall physical function in older adults.

Home Safety Assessment

Remove tripping hazards, improve lighting, install grab bars in bathrooms, and ensure pathways are clear to create a safe living environment that prevents falls.
